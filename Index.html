Critical Issues

🔥 Issue 01 — Wildfire

Escalating Bushfires

The 2019–20 "Black Summer" burned over 19 million hectares, destroyed 3,000+ homes and killed an estimated 3 billion animals. Climate change is making fire seasons longer, hotter, and more intense — with recovery windows shrinking between events.

Severity
95%
🪸 Issue 02 — Ocean

Great Barrier Reef Collapse

The world's largest coral reef system has suffered five mass bleaching events since 1998. Warming seas cause coral to expel symbiotic algae and die. Over 50% of coral cover has been lost, and projections suggest catastrophic collapse by 2050 without dramatic emissions cuts.

Severity
90%
🌿 Issue 03 — Land

Land Clearing & Habitat Loss

Australia ranks among the world's worst land-clearing nations. Queensland and NSW clear hundreds of thousands of hectares annually, fragmenting habitat corridors for koalas, gliders, and other wildlife. Loss of old-growth forests is irreversible on human timescales.

Severity
85%
🦘 Issue 04 — Species

Extinction Crisis

Australia has the world's highest rate of mammal extinction. Over 100 endemic species have been lost since European settlement. Koalas were listed as endangered in 2022. Feral cats, foxes, and land clearing drive ongoing collapse of unique island species.

Severity
88%
💧 Issue 05 — Water

Murray–Darling Water Crisis

The Murray–Darling Basin — Australia's most important river system — is collapsing under agricultural over-extraction, drought, and poor water policy. Mass fish kills, algal blooms, and dry riverbeds have devastated downstream communities and ecosystems alike.

Severity
80%
🌡️ Issue 06 — Climate

Rising Temperatures & Drought

Australia has warmed by approximately 1.5°C since 1910. Droughts are becoming more frequent and severe, threatening agriculture, water security, and human health. Heatwaves regularly break records, and the window for bold climate action is narrowing fast.

A Worsening Record

1788 — Present
European Settlement & Land Clearing
Arrival of European settlers triggered the fastest mammal extinction rate ever recorded, with feral animals and widespread clearing destroying native ecosystems.
1998
First Mass Coral Bleaching Event
El Niño–driven warming caused the Great Barrier Reef's first documented mass bleaching, setting a pattern that would escalate dramatically in coming decades.
2000–2009
The Millennium Drought
A decade-long drought devastated the Murray–Darling Basin and southeast Australia, foreshadowing the prolonged dry conditions climate science had been predicting.
2016 & 2017
Back-to-Back Mass Bleaching Events
Two consecutive bleaching years killed approximately 50% of the shallow-water coral on the northern Great Barrier Reef — unprecedented in recorded history.
2019–2020
Black Summer Megafires
Record-breaking bushfires burned 19+ million hectares across NSW, Victoria, Queensland and South Australia, releasing 900 megatonnes of CO₂ and killing an estimated 3 billion animals.
2022
Koala Listed as Endangered
Australia's most iconic animal was officially listed as endangered across Queensland, New South Wales and the ACT — a turning point for biodiversity policy.
Now
The Decisive Decade
Scientists broadly agree: the actions taken this decade will determine whether Australia's most unique ecosystems survive — or are permanently lost to history.

What You Can Do

🗳️

Vote for Strong Climate Policy

Support candidates and parties that commit to net-zero emissions, stronger land clearing laws, and science-based environmental policy at federal and state levels.

🌱

Rewild Your Backyard

Plant native species, remove invasive plants, and create habitat corridors. Backyard biodiversity projects collectively have enormous impact on local wildlife populations.

💡

Switch to Clean Energy

Move to a green energy retailer or invest in solar panels. Reducing household emissions supports the broader transition and sends market signals to power companies.

📢

Speak Up & Organise

Join or support organisations like ACF, WWF Australia, or the Wilderness Society. Write to your local MP. Collective advocacy has driven every major environmental win.

🚗

Reduce Your Footprint

Cut red meat consumption, fly less, choose public transport, and buy less new stuff. Individual actions compound — and model the behaviour change needed culturally.

💰

Support Conservation Funding

Donate to reef restoration projects, koala care programs, or Indigenous ranger programs protecting Country. Underfunded conservation groups are on the frontlines.
